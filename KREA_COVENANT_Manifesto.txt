You visit a website, you select a model, you enter a credit card number, and two days later a box arrives. The transaction is complete. The relationship — if it existed at all — is over. Before a single component is committed to production for your project, we need to know who you are. What you do. What you are building. The vehicle. The application. The installation position. The acoustic environment. The objective. This is not bureaucracy. It is the foundation of everything that follows. A speaker sold without this information is a generic product. It may be an excellent generic product — but it is not a KREA. A KREA exists for a specific purpose, in a specific place, for a specific person. Without knowing all three, it cannot exist. Ferrari does not sell a car to anyone who can afford one. They evaluate the buyer. They protect the marque. They understand that exclusivity is not arrogance — it is quality assurance. You cannot guarantee the experience if you cannot control who participates in it. We apply the same principle to sound. Not because we are concerned with appearances, but because we are concerned with performance. A KREA transducer installed incorrectly, in the wrong environment, by someone who does not understand what they have — that is not a KREA. It is a waste of an exceptional instrument. The process begins with a conversation. Not a form. A conversation.  Just listen.

KREA   COVENANT  — 3 —  02 THE CONTRACT  MUTUAL COMMITMENT · PROTECTED ECOSYSTEM  Every KREA transducer is sold under a formal agreement. This is not a terms-and-conditions checkbox that disappears the moment the page is closed. It is a signed document that establishes the terms of the relationship between KREA and the owner. The agreement contains one clause that has no exceptions: resale without KREA authorization is not permitted. This is not a commercial restriction. It is an acoustic one. A KREA transducer is not a general-purpose driver. Its mechanical and electroacoustic parameters have been calculated for a specific vehicle, a specific installation geometry, a specific acoustic volume. Placing it in a different environment without recalibration does not produce the result it was designed for. It produces an approximation. And approximation is precisely what KREA was built to eliminate. An owner who transfers a KREA component to someone else is not passing on a product. They are passing on a customized instrument that was never optimized for the recipient’s application. The sound the new owner hears is not what KREA designed. We cannot stand behind that result. Violation of the resale clause results in permanent exclusion from the KREA ecosystem. No future purchases. No warranty service. No upgrades. This is not a punitive measure. It is a statement about what KREA is: a community of people who understand the value of what they have, and protect it accordingly. The contract is not the end of something. It is the beginning. KREA   COVENANT  — 4 —  03 BUILT FOR YOUR VEHICLE. ONLY.  CUSTOM PARAMETERS · CUSTOM GEOMETRY · YOUR COLOR  No two installations are identical. The acoustic volume behind the installation point determines the compliance loading on the driver. The angle at which the transducer fires relative to the listening position determines the high-frequency directivity requirements. The power budget of the vehicle’s amplifier determines the impedance target. The cabin dimensions determine the modes the subwoofer must work with or against. These are not variables that can be ignored and corrected by equalization. They are mechanical and physical constraints that must be addressed at the transducer level, before the signal chain begins. For every KREA installation, we receive the technical documentation of the project: the vehicle, the target installation position, the available acoustic volume, the installation angle, the amplifier specifications, the intended crossover point. From this data, we calculate the optimal Thiele-Small parameters for the application. The resonant frequency, the compliance, the moving mass, the force factor — every parameter is selected or adjusted for the specific context. The result is not a standard driver fitted to a non-standard space. It is a transducer whose entire electromechanical identity was calculated for that space, and nothing else. Because an instrument should also reflect the person who plays it, the color of the surround, the membrane, and the basket finish can be specified at the time of order. Your KREA is not only tuned to your vehicle. It looks like it was made for it. Because it was.  Just listen.

KREA   COVENANT  — 5 —  04 LIFETIME  A CARD. NOT A WARRANTY DOCUMENT.  Most manufacturers offer a warranty. Two years. Five years. Sometimes ten. The fine print defines what is covered, what voids coverage, and at what point the manufacturer’s responsibility ends and yours begins. KREA offers something different. Every owner receives a KREA card. It does not expire. It is not conditional on registration, on original purchase proof, on the absence of cosmetic damage. It is a lifetime guarantee — unconditional, permanent, and personal. What the card represents is not just a repair promise. It is membership in a development relationship. A KREA transducer is not a finished product. It is the current state of an ongoing engineering effort. The driver you receive today will be improved. The motor geometry will be refined. The suspension chemistry will be optimized. The voice coil winding will evolve. These are not hypothetical future products. They are the next iterations of what you already own. When a meaningful improvement becomes available, KREA card holders have access to it at cost — not at market price, not with a margin. The cost of the materials and the work, nothing more. You ship your transducer to us. We upgrade it. We ship it back. The instrument in your vehicle is now better than it was, without replacing it. This is not a service. It is the continuation of a commitment that began the moment the contract was signed.  Just listen.

KREA   COVENANT  — 6 —  05 THE SPECIALIST  MAXIMUM PERFORMANCE · GUARANTEED. ALWAYS.  Installation is the last variable. Every decision made upstream — the parameter optimization, the cabinet geometry, the crossover point — is expressed or undermined at the moment of installation and calibration. A perfectly engineered driver installed without precision measurement is not a KREA. It is a component waiting to be properly deployed. Every KREA installation has access to a certified KREA specialist. This is not a technician with a manufacturer’s sticker on their toolbox. It is a person who has worked directly with the KREA engineering process, who understands the transducer parameters, who knows what the driver was optimized for and what the room is asking of it. The specialist arrives with measurement equipment, not assumptions. The frequency response is measured at the listening position. The time alignment is verified. The crossover is optimized for the specific acoustic behavior of the vehicle cabin. The equalization, if required, is applied with full knowledge of the driver’s linear range and the cabin’s modal behavior. The result is not a good approximation of what the KREA was designed to do. It is exactly what it was designed to do. The promise is not “you will be satisfied.” The promise is maximum performance. Measurable. Verifiable. Guaranteed. Not once. Always. Every time the specialist visits, the system is re-verified and re-optimized if the vehicle, the conditions, or the owner’s preferences have changed. Because a relationship does not end at installation.  Just listen.

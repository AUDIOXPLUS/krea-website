CREATIONS — SIGNATURE MODAL

01 — Underhung Motor

BEFORE
90% of high-end loudspeakers use an overhung motor — long coil, short gap. The moment the coil moves, windings exit the gap, the Bl force factor degrades, and distortion rises. The SIGNATURE S6 motor is underhung with 30 mm of linear excursion. The S3 achieves 15 mm — unequaled in any 3" loudspeaker. Distortion at full excursion remains below 0.5% THD, where an equivalent overhung exceeds 2–5%.
AFTER
The SIGNATURE motor is underhung: the voice coil is shorter than the magnetic gap, remaining fully immersed throughout its entire excursion. The S6 achieves 30 mm of linear excursion with distortion below 0.5% THD. The S3 achieves 15 mm in a 3-inch format. The result is consistent, linear force across every millimeter of travel.

02 — Radial Magnetization

BEFORE
Conventional motors use axial magnetization — the magnet beneath the coil, flux rising upward. To achieve 30 mm of excursion, the motor becomes a tower. SIGNATURE uses radial magnetization: magnets at the sides of the gap, flux crossing horizontally. 30 mm of excursion in a depth that conventional motors use for 8 mm. A 6.5" driver with the bass performance of a 10" subwoofer.
AFTER
SIGNATURE uses radial magnetization: magnets positioned at the sides of the gap, with flux crossing horizontally. This architecture achieves 30 mm of excursion in a remarkably compact depth — giving a 6.5-inch driver bass performance that would traditionally require a much larger format.

03 — Space-Alloy

BEFORE
Every high-end loudspeaker on the market uses mild steel in the magnetic circuit — a material that saturates at approximately 2.0 Tesla. SIGNATURE uses an alloy developed for aerospace and nuclear applications, with saturation well beyond steel limits. Combined with N55 magnets — the highest grade of neodymium commercially available. The motor assembly material cost alone exceeds the retail price of many complete loudspeakers.
AFTER
The magnetic circuit uses an alloy developed for aerospace and nuclear applications, with magnetic saturation well beyond conventional steel limits. Combined with N55 neodymium — the highest commercially available grade — the motor delivers extraordinary flux density with minimal waste.

05 — Zero Glue Motor

BEFORE
In virtually every loudspeaker motor, components are joined with adhesive. Every adhesive joint is a magnetic discontinuity with permeability close to air. The SIGNATURE motor is zero glue — components precision-machined and mechanically assembled with zero-thickness interfaces. No adhesive between magnet and backplate, no adhesive between topplate and pole. The circuit behaves exactly as simulation predicts.
AFTER
The SIGNATURE motor is assembled without adhesive. Components are precision-machined and mechanically joined with zero-thickness interfaces. No magnetic discontinuity between magnet and backplate, no gap between topplate and pole. The magnetic circuit behaves exactly as simulation predicts.

10 — Glass Crystal Cone

BEFORE
Stiffness-to-mass ratio that conventional materials cannot equal. A cone must move as a rigid piston without flexing. Paper is light but flexible. Aluminum is rigid but heavy with violent breakup. Glass crystal has high stiffness, contained mass, and manageable distributed breakup. Non-conventional cone-to-coil attachment geometry eliminates the weak point.
AFTER
Glass crystal offers an exceptional stiffness-to-mass ratio — high rigidity, contained mass, and manageable distributed breakup. The cone moves as a rigid piston without flexing, preserving the integrity of the signal. A non-conventional cone-to-coil attachment geometry reinforces the most critical junction.

12 — Xmax = Xmech

BEFORE
In SIGNATURE, Xmax and Xmech coincide. No hidden margin, no gray zone between "linear" and "mechanical." Every single millimeter of excursion is linear and usable. When others declare 15 mm Xmech and 6 mm Xmax, the remaining 9 mm exist only to prevent self-destruction — not to produce music. We have no spare millimeters. Every millimeter produces music.
AFTER
In SIGNATURE, Xmax and Xmech coincide. Every millimeter of excursion is linear and usable. There is no gap between the linear range and the mechanical limit — no reserve dedicated to survival rather than sound. Every millimeter produces music.

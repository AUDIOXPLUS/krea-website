Signature Series

S6 & S3 — Engineering Without Compromise

01 — Underhung Motor

The vast majority of high-end loudspeakers use an overhung motor — long coil, short gap. The moment the coil moves, windings exit the gap, the Bl force factor degrades, and distortion rises. The SIGNATURE motor is underhung without underhung limits: the S6 achieves 30 mm of linear excursion and the S3 achieves 15 mm. The result is consistent, linear force across every millimeter of travel — unequaled in any other loudspeaker.

02 — Radial Magnetization

Conventional motors use axial magnetization — the magnet beneath the coil, flux rising upward. To achieve 30 mm of excursion, the motor becomes a tower. SIGNATURE uses radial magnetization: magnets at the sides of the gap, flux crossing horizontally. 30 mm of excursion in a depth that conventional motors use for 8 mm. A 6.5" driver with the acceleration of a pro midrange and the bass performance of a 10" subwoofer.

03 — Space-Alloy Assembly · N55

High-end loudspeakers on the market use mild steel in the magnetic circuit — a material that saturates at approximately 2.0 Tesla. SIGNATURE uses materials developed for aerospace and nuclear applications, with saturation well beyond steel limits. Combined with N55 magnets — the highest grade of neodymium commercially available. The motor assembly material cost alone exceeds the retail price of many complete loudspeakers.

04 — Optimized Circular Geometry

The unusual circular geometry is not industrial design. It is a direct consequence of radial magnetization. When flux travels horizontally, cylindrical geometry is the only geometry guaranteeing uniform field distribution across 360°. Optimized via FEM simulation with successive iterations until every hot-spot and dead zone was eliminated. Every curve has a magnetic reason.

05 — Zero Glue Motor

In virtually every loudspeaker motor, components are joined with adhesive. Every adhesive joint is a magnetic discontinuity with permeability close to air. The SIGNATURE motor is zero glue — components precision-machined and mechanically assembled with zero-thickness interfaces. No adhesive between magnet and backplate, no adhesive between topplate and pole. The circuit behaves exactly as it should be.

06 — Internal Phase Plug–Radiator

At 30 mm of excursion, the sealed air chamber beneath the dust cap becomes a non-linear pneumatic spring adding distortion and dynamic compression. SIGNATURE integrates a precision phase plug–radiator that linearizes airflow and actively contributes to thermal dissipation, shaping the flow rather than simply venting it.

07 — Carbon Fiber Voice Coil

Carbon fiber former — specific stiffness superior to any polymer or metal, with no eddy currents. Edge-wound flat wire with dual winding eliminates voids between windings, maximizing conductor-to-void ratio. Vented with calibrated holes for controlled porosity — ensuring effective thermal dissipation under sustained power.

08 — No Terminals · Direct Connection

Coil leads exit directly from the former without interruption. No intermediate solder joint, no terminal to crimp, no spring connector. Zero added parasitic resistance. Zero future oxidation points. The signal path from coil to cable remains unbroken and pure.

09 — FEM Suspension · Custom Chemistry

An elastomer with chemical formulation designed specifically for this driver. Every corrugation, radius of curvature, and transition optimized via FEM to eliminate buckling, minimize self-resonances, and maintain linear compliance. A three-dimensional surface calculated to the tenth of a millimeter.

10 — Glass Crystal Cone

Stiffness-to-mass ratio that conventional materials cannot equal. A cone must move as a rigid piston without flexing. Paper is light but flexible. Aluminum, titanium, even diamond are rigid but with violent breakup. Glass crystal has high stiffness, contained mass, and manageable distributed breakup. Non-conventional cone-to-coil attachment geometry eliminates the weak point.

11 — Mechanical Spider

Organic geometry — not standard concentric corrugations, but a structural pattern distributing compliance three-dimensionally. FEM-developed for maximum compliance (30 mm demands it) while maintaining precise centering at minimum mass. The acceleration factor of a light, fast driver — the reactivity of a professional midrange — with the stroke of a subwoofer.

12 — Xmax = Xmech

In SIGNATURE, Xmax and Xmech coincide. No hidden margin, no gray zone between “linear” and “mechanical.” Every single millimeter of excursion is linear and usable. When others declare 15 mm Xmech and 6 mm Xmax, the remaining 9 mm exist only to prevent self-destruction — not to produce music. We have no spare millimeters. Every millimeter produces music.

13 — Multi-Part CNC Basket

The SIGNATURE basket is a precision assembly of multiple CNC-machined components. Each section is optimized independently — wall thickness, vent geometry, and structural rigidity are tuned where they matter most, without the compromises imposed by single-piece casting or stamping. This construction minimizes internal acoustic reflections while maintaining exceptional dimensional accuracy for coil centering.

Essential Series

E6 · E3 · E1 — Crafted Without Compromise

01 — Neodymium Overhung Motor · N55

The ESSENTIAL motor uses an overhung topology — efficient, well-understood, and capable of excellent sensitivity. The magnetic circuit uses the same aerospace alloy and N55 extreme-grade neodymium as the SIGNATURE series. Every Tesla generated by the magnet reaches the gap. Motor geometry optimized through FEM simulation to eliminate field hot-spots and dead zones.

02 — Conventional Architecture, Unconventional Execution

ESSENTIAL uses conventional solutions for a deliberate reason: because done well, they are genuinely excellent. The difference is in every decision during execution — gap tolerance, coil winding tension, suspension break-in protocol, final measurement and matching. ESSENTIAL is not trying to be SIGNATURE. It exists for those who want the best within conventional architecture — executed by the same hands.

03 — Hand-Treated Carbon Fiber Cone

Each cone is treated individually by hand — a process that cannot be automated. The treatment controls surface porosity, stiffness distribution, and adhesion for the coil-to-cone joint. No two cones receive identical treatment because no two cones are identical before treatment. Matched pairs measure within tolerances that automated production lines cannot reliably achieve.

04 — Carbon Fiber Voice Coil · Cone as Heatsink

Carbon fiber former — same material as SIGNATURE. Creates direct thermal continuity with the carbon fiber cone. The cone itself functions as a radiating heatsink: heat from the coil windings travels through the former into the cone body and is dissipated by the moving air. A deliberate thermal design increasing power handling without adding mass.

05 — Suspension System

Each surround and spider is measured for compliance before assembly. Combined suspension stiffness is matched to motor and moving mass within tolerances tighter than most manufacturers apply to their entire production run. Drivers outside tolerance are disassembled and rebuilt — not adjusted. Tooled components produced to our specification, not catalog parts resized to fit.

06 — Multi-Part CNC Basket · No Terminals · Direct Connection

The ESSENTIAL basket is not a single casting. It is a precision assembly of multiple CNC-machined components joined together. This multi-part construction allows each section to be optimized independently — wall thickness, vent geometry, and structural rigidity are tuned where they matter most, without the compromises imposed by single-piece casting or stamping. The result is a basket that minimizes internal acoustic reflections while maintaining exceptional dimensional accuracy for coil centering. Like the SIGNATURE, ESSENTIAL has no terminals — coil leads exit directly from the former without interruption. Zero parasitic resistance. Zero future oxidation points. Every unit hand-assembled, coil centered by hand, verified with shim gauge, tested for rub-and-buzz before curing.

07 — Crossover Compatibility

The E6, E3, and E1 were designed together as a three-way system from day one. Impedance curves, sensitivity, and frequency response optimized not for individual performance alone, but for interaction at crossover points. The crossover does less work — because the drivers were built to hand off correctly, not forced into compatibility by steep filter slopes.

08 — The Essential Promise

Same aerospace material. Same N55 neodymium. Same FEM simulation process. Same carbon fiber former. Same no-terminal direct connection. What changes is the motor topology — overhung rather than underhung. Same hands, same care, same dedication. The Essential is not a lesser product — it is a different path to the same destination: music, reproduced with honesty. Although others consider this kind of speaker their highest achievement, we consider it our starting point.

MONOLITE

Engineering Without Compromise — In the space of a book.

01 — The Impossible Box

120 × 330 × 180 millimeters. No conventional subwoofer architecture can produce genuine low-frequency extension in a volume this compact. The MONOLITE enclosure is the result of a system design where transducer and box were conceived simultaneously, optimized as a single inseparable unit. Internal acoustic volume of 1.75 litres — every cubic millimeter accounted for.

02 — Unconventional Architecture

Ports, passive radiators, transmission lines — each promises something for nothing and extracts a price. Ported enclosures borrow bass from resonant energy with compromised timing and transient attack. Passive radiators add mass and introduce their own resonant behavior. Transmission lines demand volume that compact enclosures cannot provide. The MONOLITE uses a proprietary architecture where every note is the direct consequence of transducer motion, not a resonant auxiliary system. The bass begins and ends precisely when the recording does.

03 — Zero Transmission

Every loudspeaker generates reaction force transmitted into the enclosure and room structure — muddying the acoustic image with bass that comes from everywhere and nowhere. The MONOLITE uses an internal architecture that eliminates net reaction force entirely. Place it on a glass shelf — the structure will not vibrate. The bass originates at the membranes, and from nowhere else.

04 — 34 mm of Linear Excursion

In a compact enclosure, extraordinary excursion compensates for limited radiating surface. This is physical necessity, not design preference. 34 mm peak-to-peak linear excursion — not mechanical travel, not the threshold of survival. Force factor, suspension compliance, and electrical inductance remain within a tightly controlled window across the entire stroke. What goes in, comes out.

05 — Distortion Below Perception

Flat Bl(x) — approaching the theoretical ideal of a perfectly uniform field. Flat Cms(x) — substantially flat across the full working range as a design requirement, not an approximation. Controlled Le(x) — below perceptual significance. The bass you hear is the bass that was recorded. Nothing added. Nothing subtracted.

06 — 28 Hz

A five-string bass guitar reaches 31 Hz. A concert grand piano descends to 27.5 Hz. Cinema LFE operates below 30 Hz. A subwoofer that reaches 28 Hz from 120 × 330 × 180 mm is doing something categorically different. Motor, suspension, acoustic volume, and enclosure behavior were engineered simultaneously for a single objective. The MONOLITE does not lose music below 30 Hz.

07 — The Cabinet

Each panel treated as an acoustic element with its own resonance analysis. Wall geometry, bracing, and material damping push all structural resonances outside the operating range. Compact dimensions provide an inherent advantage: shorter panel spans mean higher natural resonant frequencies. Combined with Zero Transmission, the result is a subwoofer that can be placed on any surface without that surface participating in the reproduction.

08 — Xmax = Xmech

The measured displacement limits confirm that the protection threshold coincides with the point where the force factor departs from its linear range. No gray zone. No region of technically-operating-but-already-distorting travel. No spare millimeters dedicated to survivability rather than music. Every millimeter of excursion produces linear acoustic output. Every millimeter is music.

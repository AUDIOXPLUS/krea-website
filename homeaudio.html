06 — Home Audio

The Room Is the Instrument

Where architecture becomes acoustics.

The audio industry treats the listening room as a problem to be solved — an obstacle between the speaker and the listener. We see it differently. The room is not the enemy. It is the largest acoustic component in the system. The most powerful. And the most ignored.

KREA Home Audio does not place speakers in a room. It designs an acoustic system where the room, the transducers, and the acoustic treatment are conceived together — as a single, inseparable instrument. The walls become acoustic loading surfaces. The corners become horn extensions. The boundaries become allies, not adversaries.

This is the same philosophy that guides every KREA creation. In a vehicle, the doors, the dashboard, the cabin volume are the acoustic environment — and the transducer is calculated for that specific environment. In a home, the room itself takes that role. The scale changes. The principle does not.

The Architecture

Four Pillars of Integrated Design

Boundary Loading

Conventional speakers fight the room boundaries. Our transducers are designed for specific positions relative to walls, floor, and ceiling — using the reinforcement of these surfaces as acoustic gain. The corner becomes a horn mouth. The wall becomes a baffle extension. Physics that others try to suppress, we engineer into the design.

The Room as Cabinet

A loudspeaker cabinet defines the acoustic loading of the driver. In a KREA Home system, the room itself assumes that role. The listening space — its volume, its geometry, its surfaces — is calculated as the enclosure. The transducers are engineered for this specific acoustic volume, not for an abstract anechoic specification.

Double Bass Array

Eight subwoofers, strategically positioned: four on the front wall, four on the rear wall as a cancellation array. This is not about power. It is about control. The DBA eliminates room modes at their source — creating uniform bass response across every listening position, without the artifacts of equalization or the compromises of passive treatment alone.

Integrated Treatment

Acoustic treatment is not an afterthought. It is designed simultaneously with the reproduction system — absorption, diffusion, and reflection calculated together with transducer positioning, crossover architecture, and boundary coupling. The treatment does not fix problems. It completes the instrument.

Flagship

Home Signature

The room, reimagined. A complete acoustic architecture.

The Signature tier is a total intervention. The listening room is designed from the ground up — or redesigned from an existing space — as a purpose-built acoustic instrument. Every surface, every dimension, every material is part of the reproduction system.

KREA Signature Transducers

Full-range and midrange reproduction using KREA Signature drivers — calculated for boundary-coupled positions within your specific room geometry. The same underhung motor, glass crystal cone, and zero-glue architecture, optimized for architectural acoustic loading.

8-Unit Double Bass Array

Complete DBA implementation with eight dedicated subwoofers. Front array generates the wavefront. Rear array absorbs room-reflected energy. The result: flat bass response independent of listening position, with transient accuracy that passive treatment cannot achieve.

Architectural Acoustic Treatment

Full room treatment designed as part of the system — custom absorbers, diffusers, and reflective surfaces engineered for the specific room dimensions and the specific transducer positions. Materials, geometry, and placement calculated together. The room becomes sonically transparent where it must, and acoustically supportive where it should.

System Calibration

Multi-point acoustic measurement and calibration. Crossover architecture designed for the combined transducer-room system. DSP integration where beneficial, analog signal path where possible. The system is tuned not to a curve, but to musical truth in that specific space.

Core

Home Essential

The same philosophy. Applied to your existing space.

Not every project requires — or allows — a complete architectural intervention. The Essential tier applies the same integrated design philosophy to an existing room, with less invasive modifications that respect the space while transforming its acoustic character.

KREA Essential Transducers

Boundary-optimized reproduction using KREA Essential drivers. Positioned and calculated for the acoustic characteristics of your existing room — exploiting favorable boundary interactions while managing the unavoidable ones. The same engineering methodology, adapted to real-world constraints.

Targeted Bass Management

Strategic subwoofer placement calculated for your room modes. Where a full DBA is not feasible, we employ optimized multi-sub configurations that address the most significant modal problems — achieving substantial improvement without requiring structural modifications to the rear wall.

Targeted Acoustic Treatment

Strategic placement of absorption and diffusion at critical reflection points and modal hotspots. Designed to integrate visually with the existing room while delivering measurable acoustic improvement. Less material, more precision — every panel justified by measurement.

System Integration

Complete system design including crossover architecture, amplification recommendations, and DSP calibration. Measured, tuned, and verified in your room. The Essential tier is not a simplified version of the Signature. It is the same philosophy, applied with surgical precision to a different starting point.

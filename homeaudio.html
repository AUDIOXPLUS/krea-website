06 — Home Audio

The Room Is the Instrument

Where architecture becomes acoustics.

The audio industry treats the listening room as a problem to be solved — an obstacle between the speaker and the listener. We see it differently. The room is not the enemy. It is the largest acoustic component in the system. The most powerful. And the most ignored.

KREA Home Audio does not place speakers in a room. It designs an acoustic system where the room, the transducers, and the acoustic treatment are conceived together — as a single, inseparable instrument. The walls become acoustic loading surfaces. The corners become horn extensions. The boundaries become allies, not adversaries.

This is the same philosophy that guides every KREA creation. In a vehicle, the doors, the dashboard, the cabin volume are the acoustic environment — and the transducer is calculated for that specific environment. In a home, the room itself takes that role. The scale changes. The principle does not.

The Architecture

Four Pillars of Integrated Design

Boundary Loading

Conventional speakers fight the room boundaries. Our transducers are designed for specific positions relative to walls, floor, and ceiling — using the reinforcement of these surfaces as acoustic gain. The corner becomes a horn mouth. The wall becomes a baffle extension. Physics that others try to suppress, we engineer into the design.

The Room as Cabinet

A loudspeaker cabinet defines the acoustic loading of the driver. In a KREA Home system, the room itself assumes that role. The listening space — its volume, its geometry, its surfaces — is calculated as the enclosure. The transducers are engineered for this specific acoustic volume, not for an abstract anechoic specification.

Distributed Bass Perimeter

Dozens of ultra-flat subwoofer modules distributed around the room perimeter — integrated into the base of the walls. Each module uses KREA’s proprietary Zero Transmission Architecture: force-cancelling driver pairs that produce no structural vibration. With the workload shared across the array, each unit operates at a fraction of its capability. Distortion approaches zero. Room modes are controlled at their source — eliminating the need for meter-thick bass treatment panels.

Integrated Treatment

Acoustic treatment is not an afterthought. It is designed simultaneously with the reproduction system — absorption, diffusion, and reflection calculated together with transducer positioning, crossover architecture, and boundary coupling. The treatment does not fix problems. It completes the instrument.

The System

Five Modules. One Instrument.

Every KREA Home system is composed of modules that scale to the room and the project. The number of each module is calculated for the specific space — no two systems are alike. Each module builds on the previous, from the foundation of bass to the final acoustic refinement of the room itself.

01

Bass Perimeter

Ultra-flat Zero Transmission subwoofer modules, integrated at the base of the walls. The number of units is calculated for the room — from 10 to 50 modules. More modules mean lower distortion, deeper extension, and flatter modal response. The foundation of everything.

02

Midbass — S6

KREA Signature S6 transducers handle the midbass — from the Bass Perimeter crossover up to the midrange handoff. The number of S6 units depends on the Bass Perimeter configuration: more perimeter modules allow a higher crossover point, reducing the demands on each S6.

03

Mid-High — S3 + S1

KREA Signature S3 midrange and S1 tweeter complete the frequency range. Positioned and calculated for the acoustic geometry of the room. The same underhung motor, glass crystal cone, and zero-glue architecture — optimized for architectural acoustic loading.

04

Room Reinforcement

Custom acoustic treatment designed for the specific combination of room and modules installed. Absorption, diffusion, and reflection surfaces calculated and positioned to complete the acoustic environment. Every panel justified by measurement.

05

Wall Integration

The ultimate option. Purpose-built walls transform the room into the system itself. Baffle wall with flush-mounted transducers eliminates reflections at the source. Air-gap construction on lateral and rear walls provides broadband absorption without meter-thick panels. The Bass Perimeter disappears into the wall base. The room becomes the instrument.

Flagship

Home Signature

The room, reimagined. A complete acoustic architecture.

The Signature tier is a total intervention. The listening room is designed from the ground up — or redesigned from an existing space — as a purpose-built acoustic instrument. Wall Integration, Bass Perimeter, flush-mounted KREA Signature transducers, and architectural acoustic treatment — conceived as a single system.

Bass Perimeter

Dozens of Zero Transmission subwoofer modules integrated into the base of purpose-built walls. Invisible from the room. Each force-cancelling pair produces no structural vibration — the walls remain silent. The distributed array controls room modes across all dimensions simultaneously, with each unit operating at a fraction of a millimeter of excursion. Distortion below the threshold of measurement.

KREA Signature — Flush Mounted

S6, S3, and S1 transducers flush-mounted into the front baffle wall. No cabinet reflections. No SBIR. The same underhung motor, glass crystal cone, and zero-glue architecture — with Thiele-Small parameters calculated for the acoustic loading of your specific room geometry and wall construction.

Wall Integration

Purpose-built walls with air-gap construction. The gap between the structural wall and the interior surface places absorptive material where particle velocity is highest — achieving broadband bass absorption that conventional surface-mounted panels cannot. Every wall becomes an acoustic element. The room becomes sonically transparent where it must, and supportive where it should.

System Calibration

Multi-point acoustic measurement and calibration verified at the listening position. The audio chain — source, amplification, crossover architecture — is reviewed and approved by KREA to ensure it honors the acoustic design. The system is tuned not to a curve, but to musical truth in that specific space.

Core

Home Essential

The same philosophy. In your existing space.

Not every project requires — or allows — a complete architectural intervention. The Essential tier brings the same modular philosophy into an existing room. No structural modifications. The modules are placed, positioned, measured, and calibrated — transforming the acoustic character of the space without altering its architecture.

Bass Perimeter — Freestanding

The same Zero Transmission subwoofer modules, placed directly on the floor along the room perimeter. At seven centimeters tall, they disappear beneath the sight line. No structural modification required. The number of modules is calculated for your room — even a modest array delivers modal control that conventional subwoofers cannot approach.

KREA Essential Transducers

E6, E3, and E1 transducers in standalone enclosures, positioned according to KREA boundary-loading calculations for your specific room. Exploiting favorable wall and floor interactions rather than fighting them. The same engineering methodology, in a format that requires no construction.

Targeted Acoustic Treatment

Panels at first reflection points, bass management at critical corners. Designed to integrate visually with the existing room while delivering measurable acoustic improvement. Less material, more precision — every panel justified by measurement, every position calculated.

System Integration

Complete system design including crossover architecture, amplification, and calibration — measured, tuned, and verified in your room. The audio chain is reviewed and approved by KREA. A certified positioning plan. The Essential tier is not a simplified Signature. It is the same philosophy, applied to a different starting point.
